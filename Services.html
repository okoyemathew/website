  • Bone Tumor (Neoplasm): An abnormal growth of cells in the bone, which can be benign or malignant, potentially causing pain, swelling, and weakened bones.

surgery

  • Arthroscopy: A minimally invasive procedure using a tiny camera to diagnose and treat joint issues, often in the knee, shoulder, or hip. It allows for faster recovery compared to open surgery.
  • Hip Replacement: A surgical procedure where a damaged hip joint is replaced with an artificial one, relieving pain and restoring mobility for patients with severe arthritis or injury.
  • Arthroplasty: A joint replacement surgery aimed at restoring function to a damaged joint by replacing it with a prosthesis, commonly performed on the hip, knee, or shoulder.
  • Arthroscopy: Similar to arthroscopy, but generally involves joint repair or cleanup through small incisions using specialized tools for minimally invasive joint care.

Management

  • Diabetes Management: A comprehensive approach to controlling blood sugar levels, including lifestyle changes, medication, and regular monitoring. Key aspects involve a balanced diet, regular exercise, and adherence to prescribed insulin or oral medications.
